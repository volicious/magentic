# Definitions of Ready by Artifact

Not all work requires the same level of documentation before implementation.

The required level of readiness depends on the type of work being performed.

---

# Initiative Definition of Ready

An Initiative is ready when:

- Business objective is clearly defined.
- Strategic value is understood.
- Success metrics are identified.
- Executive sponsor has been identified.
- Major stakeholders have been identified.
- Initial risks have been documented.
- Initial assumptions have been documented.
- Major dependencies have been identified.
- Product Manager has accepted ownership.

The purpose of an Initiative DoR is to determine whether the organization should invest in solving the problem.

---

# Epic Definition of Ready

An Epic is ready when:

- Business objective is clear.
- Scope boundaries are defined.
- Business value is documented.
- Success metrics are defined.
- Major workflows are understood.
- Stakeholders have been identified.
- Major business rules have been identified.
- High-level risks have been documented.
- High-level dependencies have been identified.
- Product Manager has approved the Epic.

The purpose of an Epic DoR is to determine whether discovery is sufficiently complete to begin feature decomposition.

---

# Feature Definition of Ready

A Feature is ready when:

- Functional requirements are documented.
- Major workflows are complete.
- Business rules are documented.
- Integrations have been identified.
- Reporting requirements are understood.
- Security considerations have been documented.
- Dependencies have owners.
- Risks have been documented.
- UX requirements are understood.
- Architecture review has been completed (when required).
- Product Manager has approved the Feature.

The purpose of a Feature DoR is to determine whether implementation planning can begin.

---

# User Story Definition of Ready

A User Story is ready when:

- Business objective is clear.
- Story scope is appropriately sized.
- Acceptance criteria are complete.
- Requirements are testable.
- Edge cases have been documented.
- Business rules have been identified.
- Dependencies have been documented.
- Risks have been documented.
- Assumptions have been documented.
- Open questions have been resolved or assigned.
- QA confirms the story is testable.
- Engineering confirms sufficient information exists to estimate implementation effort.

The purpose of a User Story DoR is to ensure Engineering can begin implementation with minimal clarification.

---

# Bug Definition of Ready

A Bug is ready for Engineering when:

- Problem description is complete.
- Expected behavior is documented.
- Actual behavior is documented.
- Reproduction steps are documented.
- Severity has been assigned.
- Business impact has been documented.
- Affected users have been identified.
- Environment has been documented.
- Supporting evidence has been attached when available.
- Current workarounds have been documented.
- Potential workarounds have been documented (if known).
- Root cause status has been documented (if known).
- Product Manager has confirmed the priority.

The purpose of a Bug DoR is to ensure Engineering can efficiently reproduce, diagnose, and resolve the defect.

---

# Guiding Principle

Every artifact should contain enough information for the next role in the product development lifecycle to perform their work confidently.

Readiness should be proportional to the size, complexity, and risk of the work.

Small work should remain lightweight.

Large work should receive deeper analysis.

The objective is not to create documentation.

The objective is to reduce uncertainty before implementation begins.
